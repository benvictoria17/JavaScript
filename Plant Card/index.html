Description Stinging nettle is a perennial flowering plant in the family Urticaceae. It is native to Europe, Asia, northern Africa, and North America and has been introduced to other parts of the world. The plant has green, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The stems and leaves are covered with tiny, needle-like hairs that contain irritants and cause a stinging sensation when touched. Stinging nettle has been used medicinally for centuries to treat a wide variety of ailments, including joint pain, skin irritation, and allergies. It is also used as a food source in some cultures .
